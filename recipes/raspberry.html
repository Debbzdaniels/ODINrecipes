Blended Raspberry Chia Pudding

Description.

If you love the idea of chia pudding but do not like the chunky texture, this recipe is for you! Blending chia pudding turns it into a smooth, almost mousse-like texture.
This one is flavored with juicy, tart raspberries which gives it a dreamy pink color too!

Ingredients.

  • 8 tbsp chia seeds
  • 480ml milk of choice
  • 3 tbsp monkfruit erythritol sweetener
  • 1/2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 125g fresh or frozen raspberries.

Steps.

  1. Place all ingredients in a high speed blender.
    Blend until mixture is completely smooth and creamy.
  2. Transfer to 4 mason jars or containers with lids.
    Cover and refrigerate for 2 hours or overnight.
  3. When ready to serve, top with granola and more
    fresh raspberries or any other toppings of choice. Enjoy!

Pro-tips.

  • Don’t scale down the recipe otherwise you won’t have enough volume for the blender to blend the mixture properly.
  • Blended chia pudding will keep in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 5 days.
  • You may substitute monkfruit erythritol sweetener with any other sweetener of choice.
  • I used soy milk but feel free to use any milk of choice.
